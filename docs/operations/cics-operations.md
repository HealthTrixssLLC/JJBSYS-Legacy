# CICS Operations

This page highlights the operational emphasis found across the archive. It is not a runbook or installation guide.

## Business View

Operational tooling is often invisible when it works well, but it is one of the main reasons business systems stay dependable. These members point to an environment where uptime, supportability, controlled startup and shutdown, queue health, and region discipline were treated as business-critical capabilities.

## Common Themes

- Files and queues
- Terminal management
- PLT startup and shutdown
- User exits and region hooks
- Monitoring and alerts
- Refresh, reload, and region management

## Problems This Helped Solve

- Reducing downtime during startup, shutdown, or region maintenance.
- Giving support teams tools to inspect and manage live operational conditions.
- Improving recovery and control when queues, files, or sessions became problematic.
- Making mission-critical transaction systems easier to operate safely at scale.

## Why This Was A Strength

- It reflects operational maturity, not just application coding.
- It recognizes that successful business systems need tooling around them, not only business transactions inside them.
- It reduces reliance on fragile manual work by turning recurring operational needs into repeatable utilities.

## How To Read These Members

Look for resource names, transaction IDs, startup hooks, status reporting, and support utilities that appear designed to help operators or systems programmers manage a live CICS environment.
