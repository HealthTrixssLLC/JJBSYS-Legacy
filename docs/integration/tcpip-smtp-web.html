The archive includes patterns related to TCP/IP sockets, SMTP email, HTTP-style exchange, UPS and EDI-style integrations, and CICS web analyzer, converter, and state-management components.

Business View

This part of the archive represents the ability to connect a stable mainframe core to the outside world. That matters because systems create more business value when they can exchange information, trigger notifications, and participate in broader workflows.

Any real endpoints, credentials, certificates, keyrings, partner identifiers, and mail settings must be supplied locally and should never be stored in Git.

Problems this helped solve

It helped connect core transaction systems to partners, notifications, and newer workflow channels without discarding the mainframe core.

Why it was innovative

It reflects a practical modernization instinct: keep the trusted system, but expand how it can participate in enterprise operations.

Strengths

Adaptability, business reach, and reuse of trusted transaction logic are clear strengths in this area.
